# Stakeholder Analysis - Student Assignment Tracker System

The following table identifies key stakeholders involved in the system, along with their roles, concerns, challenges, and measurable success criteria.

| Stakeholder | Role | Key Concerns | Pain Points | Success Metrics |
|------------|------|-------------|------------|----------------|
| Student | Primary end-user responsible for managing assignments and deadlines | Ability to easily track assignments, view deadlines, and stay organized | Missed deadlines, poor task organization, lack of reminders | At least 90% of assignments submitted on time |
| Lecturer | Provides assignments and sets academic deadlines for students | Ensuring students are aware of deadlines and submit work on time | High number of late or missed submissions | Reduction in late submissions by at least 30% |
| System Administrator | Responsible for maintaining system availability and performance | System reliability, uptime, and smooth operation | System downtime, performance issues, and service interruptions | System availability of 99% or higher |
| Developer | Designs, develops, and maintains the system | Code quality, maintainability, and clear system requirements | Ambiguous requirements and complex system logic | Reduction in bugs and faster implementation of new features |
| University Management | Oversees academic performance and student success | Improved student productivity and academic outcomes | Low pass rates due to poor time management by students | Measurable improvement in student success rates |
| IT Support Staff | Provides technical assistance and resolves system issues | System stability, ease of troubleshooting, and user support efficiency | High volume of user complaints and recurring technical issues | Reduction in support requests by at least 40% |
